Bill Gates

The Man Who Put a Computer on Every Desk

Here's a timeline of Bill Gates's life:

  • 1955 — Born on October 28 in Seattle, Washington.
  • 1967 — Enrolls at the private Lakeside School, where he first discovers the computer and writes his first software program (a tic-tac-toe game) at age 13. He meets his future Microsoft co-founder, Paul Allen, here.
  • 1973 — Enrolls at Harvard University. He scores 1590 out of 1600 on the SAT.
  • 1975 — Drops out of Harvard after his sophomore year to co-found Micro-Soft (the hyphen was later dropped) with Paul Allen in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Their first product is BASIC software for the Altair 8800 microcomputer.
  • 1980 — Microsoft signs a pivotal deal with IBM to provide an operating system for the new IBM PC. Microsoft purchases an existing OS, modifies it, and licenses it to IBM as MS-DOS, a move that catapults the company to prominence.
  • 1985 — Microsoft Windows 1.0 is released, providing a graphical user interface (GUI) that makes personal computing far more accessible to the average person.
  • 1986 — Microsoft goes public with an Initial Public Offering (IPO). Gates, at 30, instantly becomes one of the world's youngest paper billionaires.
  • 1994 — Marries Melinda French, a Microsoft product manager, in Hawaii.
  • 1995 — Releases his book, The Road Ahead, discussing the implications of the future internet, and becomes the richest man in the world (a title he would hold for many years).
  • 1998 — Microsoft faces an antitrust lawsuit from the U.S. Justice Department, alleging the company abused its monopoly position with the Windows operating system.
  • 2000 — Steps down as CEO and, with his wife, co-founds the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, focusing initially on global health and education. He transitions to the role of Chief Software Architect at Microsoft.
  • 2006 — Announces he will transition out of his full-time role at Microsoft to dedicate more time to the Foundation, which focuses on eradicating diseases like polio and malaria.
  • 2010 — Co-founds The Giving Pledge with Warren Buffett, a commitment by the world's wealthiest individuals to dedicate the majority of their wealth to philanthropy.
  • 2015 — Launches Breakthrough Energy Coalition, an initiative to spur investment in clean energy technologies, emphasizing his shift toward addressing climate change.
  • 2020 — Steps down from the Microsoft and Berkshire Hathaway boards to dedicate more time to the Foundation's work, including its response to the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • 2021 — Announces his divorce from Melinda French Gates after 27 years of marriage. He continues his work as co-chair of the Foundation.
  • 2022 — Releases his book, How to Prevent the Next Pandemic, drawing on lessons learned during the COVID-19 crisis.
  • 2023 — Speaks extensively about the revolutionary potential of Artificial Intelligence in areas like health and education.
